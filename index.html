About

Niki Murphy is a recent graduate from MICA with a BFA in Film & Video. As an artist, filmmaker, and event curator, she dedicates herself to innovative storytelling with a focus on Korean culture and intercultural understanding through film and media. She's worked on the sets of multiple films, co-founded Baltimore's first Korean Film Festival SEEK, and in 2015 co-founded the interdisciplinary project "In The Doorway" documenting the cultural intersections of Korea and Baltimore through an exhibition, film, screenings, and printed catalogue.

She currently works as a video editor and production assistant for Group SJR, a content and branding company in Manhattan.

SEEK

See Korea, Baltimore’s Korean Film Festival

Over the course of two nights, SEEK introduces Korean film & culture to the Baltimore community through screenings of Korean and Korean-American short and feature length films, performances, and discussions. In 2016 SEEK screened a 35mm print of “Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter... and Spring” by Korean arthouse director Ki-duk Kim and “Treeless Mountain” film by a Korean-American director So Young Kim.

We believe that cinema is a catalyst for change and aim to increase our awareness andcuriosity of the cultures that co-exist in the Baltimore.

In 2016 SEEK was funded by the Y.L. Hoi Memorial Award, in support of projects that use art to create a new paradigm for racial understanding, equity, and harmony in Baltimore.
